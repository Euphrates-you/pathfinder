01 / EARTH

GRAVITY

FALLING, FOREVER

Gravity is not a pull — it is geometry. Earth's mass bends the spacetime around it, and everything nearby follows the straightest possible path through that curve. The ISS doesn't float: it falls around the planet at 7.66 km/s, moving sideways so fast it keeps missing the ground. That is all an orbit is.

g = GM / r² ≈ 9.81 m/s²
  • MASS5.97×10²⁴ KG
  • RADIUS6 371 KM

02 / MARS

ESCAPE

THE TYRANNY OF THE ROCKET EQUATION

To leave a world you must outrun its gravity well — 11.2 km/s on Earth, just 5.0 on Mars. But rockets carry their own fuel, and fuel has mass, so fuel needs fuel: payload grows linearly, propellant exponentially. This one equation is why spaceflight is hard — and why Mars, with its shallow well, is the obvious second home.

Δv = vₑ · ln( m₀ / m₁ )
  • GRAVITY0.38 G
  • ESCAPE V5.03 KM/S
  • TRANSFER≈ 210 DAYS

03 / JUPITER

SCALE

THE FAILED STAR

Jupiter outweighs every other planet combined — two and a half times over — yet it would need 80× more mass to ignite fusion and become a star. Inside, pressure crushes hydrogen into a liquid metal ocean. The Great Red Spot is a single storm wider than Earth that has raged for at least 350 years.

M♃ = 318 M⊕ = 2.5 × Σ planets

04 / SATURN

TIDES

THE SHREDDED MOON

Gravity pulls harder on the near side of a body than the far side — that difference is the tide. Stray too close and the difference exceeds the strength of rock itself: the Roche limit. Saturn's rings are likely the wreckage of a moon that crossed that line — 282,000 km wide, yet only about ten meters thick.

d ≈ 2.44 R ( ρₚ / ρₘ )^⅓
  • RING SPAN282 000 KM
  • THICKNESS~10 M
  • DENSITY0.69 G/CM³ — IT FLOATS

05 / SOL

FUSION

MASS INTO LIGHT

Every second the Sun fuses 600 million tonnes of hydrogen into helium — and 4 million tonnes of mass simply vanish, converted to pure energy. A photon born in the core spends ~100,000 years ricocheting toward the surface. Its final leg — to your eyes — takes eight minutes.

E = mc²
  • CORE15.7 MILLION K
  • OUTPUT3.8×10²⁶ W
  • MASS → LIGHT4M TONNES/S

06 / PULSAR PSR B1919+21

COLLAPSE

A CITY-SIZED ATOM

When a massive star dies, its core collapses until electrons are crushed into protons — leaving a ball of pure neutrons twenty kilometers across. One sugar cube of it outweighs a mountain. It spins hundreds of times per second, sweeping radio beams across the galaxy like a lighthouse — a clock so stable we use it to test general relativity.

ρ ≈ 5×10¹⁷ kg/m³

  • SPIN≤ 716 REV/S

07 / GARGANTUA-CLASS SINGULARITY

SINGULARITY

WHERE TIME POINTS INWARD

Compress anything below its Schwarzschild radius and escape velocity exceeds light itself. The event horizon isn't a surface — it's a one-way boundary in time: inside, every possible future points toward the center. Watch a friend fall in and they redden, slow, and freeze at the horizon forever. To them, nothing special happens at all — until the tides pull them into spaghetti.
